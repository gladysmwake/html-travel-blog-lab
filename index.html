📍 My trip report

A barrier island
like no other

Fire Island National Seashore turned out to be everything I'd hoped for — and more. Stretched along a slender barrier island off the south shore of Long Island, the park offers an extraordinary escape from city life. Cars are completely banned, so the only way around is on foot or by bike along weathered wooden boardwalks.

I spent three sun-drenched days exploring the Sunken Forest, a rare maritime holly forest whose canopy feels primordial and otherworldly. In the evenings I wandered the Watch Hill boardwalks and watched ospreys dive into the shimmering Great South Bay. The beaches were pristine, wide and wonderfully empty.

Wildlife sightings were a daily gift — white-tailed deer at the forest edge, horseshoe crabs in the shallows, piping plovers nesting right at the dune line. Whether you come for the hiking, the birding, or simply salt air on your face, Fire Island delivers a quiet magic that's impossible to find this close to New York City.

What to do

Island highlights

🌊

Atlantic Beaches

Wide, unspoiled shorelines perfect for swimming, shelling, and watching the surf pound the coast at golden hour.

🌿

Sunken Forest

A rare maritime holly forest tucked behind the dunes — a living time capsule of old-growth coastal woodland.

🦌

Wild Deer

White-tailed deer roam freely across the island, unbothered by the occasional awestruck visitor passing on the boardwalk.

🏛️

The Lighthouse

The iconic 168-foot Fire Island Lighthouse has guided mariners since 1858. Climb it for sweeping bay-and-ocean views.
